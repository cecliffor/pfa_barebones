Conditions Treated

Your feet are the foundation of everything you do. When something feels off — whether it's a sharp pain, a strange lump, a stubborn skin issue, or a condition linked to another health problem — care is available. At Pathways Foot & Ankle, the full range of foot and ankle conditions are diagnosed and treated, from common problems like ingrown toenails and plantar fasciitis to complex deformities and systemic diseases. No referral is needed, and most treatments start conservatively, only moving to surgery when truly necessary.

Advanced In-Office Care & Services

Diagnostic Ultrasound

Painless, radiation-free imaging allows examination of soft tissues like tendons, ligaments, and nerves in real time. It's especially helpful for diagnosing plantar fasciitis, tendon tears, neuromas, and cysts.

Functional Medicine

Foot problems often stem from whole-body issues like inflammation, metabolic imbalances, or nutritional deficiencies. Functional medicine testing digs deeper to treat the root cause — not just the symptoms.

Toenail Removal

For infected toenails, chronically ingrown, thickened, or painful nails, safe in-office nail removal is available. The procedure is quick and can be made permanent if the nail edge keeps growing back.

Cortisone Injections

A powerful anti-inflammatory shot that reduces swelling and pain from conditions like arthritis, tendinitis, and plantar fasciitis. Relief often begins within days and can last for weeks or months.

Custom Orthotics

Prescription devices made specifically for your feet. Unlike over-the-counter inserts, custom orthotics correct your unique biomechanics, relieve pressure points, and prevent reinjury.

Shockwave Therapy

A non-invasive treatment that uses high-energy sound waves to stimulate healing in chronic conditions like plantar fasciitis, peripheral neuropathy, neuromas, and Achilles tendinitis. No needles, no downtime. Typically requires 3-5 sessions for maximum results.

Prolotherapy

Prolotherapy (proliferative therapy) uses a small amount of an irritant solution — typically dextrose — injected into a weakened ligament or tendon. This triggers a natural healing response, strengthening the tissue and reducing chronic pain. It's especially effective for ankle instability, plantar fasciitis, and unresolved sprains.

Homeopathic Injection Therapy

A gentle, plant-based alternative to corticosteroids and NSAIDs. This therapy is designed to reduce inflammation, break up scar tissue, and relieve nerve pain without the side effects of stronger medications. It's often used for neuromas, arthritic joints, and chronic tendinitis.

Routine Nail and Callus Care

Not just a trim — medical assessment and treatment of thick, deformed, or fungal nails, along with painful, hardened skin. Using sterile instruments, calluses are reduced to prevent ulcers and nails are carefully debrided to restore comfort and mobility. Ideal for diabetics, seniors, or anyone who struggles to care for their own feet.

Laser Therapy for Pain & Inflammation

Medical grade lasers use focused, painless light energy to penetrate deep into tissues without heat or discomfort. For acute and chronic pain, it reduces inflammation, stimulates cell repair, and improves blood flow — effectively treating arthritis, tendonitis, neuropathy, and sports injuries. Each session takes only a few minutes, and most patients notice improvement after 3–6 treatments.

Laser Therapy for Toenail Fungus

Specialized laser energy safely heats and destroys toenail fungus without damaging the surrounding skin or nail. The treatment is painless, requires no needles or anesthesia, and has no downtime.

Minimally Invasive Surgery

When surgery is needed, small incisions (often less than 1 cm) are used to correct bunions, hammertoes, neuromas, and more. The result is less pain, faster recovery, and no large scars.

Dr. Clifford completed his residency training at the Franciscan Foot and Ankle Institute in Washington, where he later served as Director of Residency Training. With more than a decade of performing, teaching, and developing surgical techniques, Dr. Clifford recognized that most foot and ankle surgery can be avoided with appropriate conservative care. In 2022, he left his successful high volume surgical practice to focus on expanding patient access to cutting-edge nonsurgical treatment—though he continues to perform surgery when necessary, utilizing the latest advancements in minimally invasive techniques. Dr. Clifford understands that there is no "one size fits all" solution in medicine, and every patient deserves an individualized, evidence-based approach, not a generic treatment plan.
